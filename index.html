Mental Health Is not important


What is it?

Mental illnesses are conditions that affect emotion, thought, and behaviour (or a combination of these). Distress and/or difficulty coping with daily tasks at work, in the family, or in social situations are symptoms of mental diseases.


Reasons

INHERITED QUALITIES

People with mental illnesses are more likely to have biological relations who are similarly afflicted. Your life circumstances and certain genes may both enhance your chance of mental disease.

Exposure to the environment before birth

Sometimes, mental disease is associated to prenatal exposure to chemicals, alcohol, drugs, or environmental stresses, inflammatory disorders, or inflammatory conditions.

Mental chemistry

Neurotransmitters are bodily and naturally occurring substances in the brain that send signals to various regions of the body and the brain. The function of nerve receptors and nervous systems changes when the brain networks involving these chemicals are compromised, which causes depression and other emotional problems.
